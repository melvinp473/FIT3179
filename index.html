Education in Australia

In the land down under, education stands as a cornerstone of society, shaping the minds of future generations and driving innovation forward. Recent data from Australian schools paints a vivid picture of how education is distributed and how it's evolving.

School Count

Firstly, school counts by state and territory reveal significant differences across the country. New South Wales, with its sprawling urban centres, leads the pack with 3,125 schools, while the Northern Territory, with its vast and sparsely populated areas, has far fewer schools. The Australian Capital Territory, being a smaller territory, has the least, with only 137 schools.

Student Enrolments

Delving into student enrolment, a snapshot from 2023 shows a diverse landscape. States like Victoria and New South Wales have high numbers of student enrolments, with both states having more than 1 million enrolments, reflecting their larger populations.

Overseas Student Enrolments

One intriguing aspect of this educational tapestry is the presence of full-fee-paying overseas students. From 2019 to 2023, these enrolments provide insight into Australia's global appeal as an education hub. States like Victoria and New South Wales attract a significant number of these students, with Victoria recording 10,499 overseas enrolments in 2019, the highest recorded, underscoring their strong international reputation.

Proportion of Enrolments

Finally, the proportion of enrolments by school affiliation over time reveals shifting trends. Since 2006, there has been a gradual increase in enrolments in independent schools, with enrolments growing by 3.3%, hinting at a growing demand for alternative education options.
